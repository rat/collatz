# E-065 — Verification of paper #049 (Boyle, "The Collatz Conjecture is True") — PROOF CLAIM

Related hypothesis: [`H-065-boyle-collatz-proof-review.md`](../../hypotheses/H-065-boyle-collatz-proof-review.md)

## Paper

Boyle, D.G. (2026). *The Collatz Conjecture is True*. rxiverse.org
(not peer-reviewed). Local PDF:
`literature/papers/049_The-Collatz-Conjecture-is-True.pdf`.

## Verdict

**NOT a valid proof.** A fatal gap located in Lemma 4.2: the paper
derives `e=2/3, o=1/3` (asymptotic fractions of even/odd steps) under
the "random n" hypothesis (Theorem 3.2 — a density/ensemble argument),
then substitutes these values directly into a claim about ONE specific,
fixed hypothetical trajectory (the one generated by `n+1`, assumed
divergent). This is the classic "random-walk argument" fallacy — useful
heuristic for intuition, not a proof.

## What was tested

1. Arithmetic of the 2-, 3-, 4-term cycle exclusions — correct.
2. Exhaustive search of the Diophantine equation `2^(n+1)-3^m=1`
   (including the `n+1` odd case, which the paper doesn't handle) — the
   paper's numerical conclusion holds, but the proof as written has a
   gap.
3. Arithmetic of the geometric series (limit = 2/3) — correct.
4. **Quantitative demonstration**: the fraction of even steps realized
   by 3,000 complete individual trajectories — standard deviation
   0.031, 10.2% deviate from 2/3 by more than 0.05, showing that "2/3"
   is an ensemble average, not a per-trajectory constraint.
