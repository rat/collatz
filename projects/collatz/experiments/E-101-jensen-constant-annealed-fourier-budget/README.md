# E-101 — Jensen constant of the annealed Fourier budget (Proposition C's wall)

Related hypothesis: [`H-127-reducao-z-number-dicotomia-espectral-wcc.md`](../../hypotheses/H-127-reducao-z-number-dicotomia-espectral-wcc.md)

## What was done

Monte Carlo calibration of the "annealed" decay exponent (independent
uniform phases — the pseudorandom benchmark) of a local Riesz-product
cascade factor of the type used in Tao's (2011) blog post
(Littlewood-Offord/powers of 2 and 3), originally generated by the
Fable during the consultation on the Z-number reduction lemma (H-127)
and reproduced/verified in this session.

Model: Z = Σ_{g≥1} w_g·e(U_g), U_g i.i.d. uniform on [0,1), normalized
geometric weights w_g = p(1-p)^{g-1}. We compute Λ := E[log(1/|Z|)] by
Monte Carlo (2×10⁶ samples, truncated at G=60 terms — the geometric
tail is already negligible), for two models:

- **Model 1** (p=1/2): Geom(1/2) weights, Tao's (2022) original i.i.d.
  model for the Syracuse measure.
- **Model 2** (p=1/γ₀, γ₀=1+log₄3≈1.7925): weights tilted to the
  critical slope of Wirsching's WCC threshold (j*/ℓ at the naive
  threshold).

The Jensen identity (E_U log|p·e(U)+(1-p)Z′| = log p whenever
|Z′|≤p/(1-p), automatic for p≥1/2 i.e. γ≤2) gives Λ=log γ **exactly**
— the Monte Carlo is a check, not the source of the value.

## Result

- Model 1: Λ = 0.6929 ± 0.0006 = ln 2 (exact, confirms the identity).
- Model 2 (WCC threshold): Λ = 0.5834 ± 0.0005 = log(1.7925) = log γ₀
  (exact, confirms the identity at the point relevant to the WCC).
- In both cases, Λ < log 3 ≈ 1.0986 — **the annealed Fourier budget fits
  with no extra structure needed**: a spectrally diffuse hole is
  self-consistent from the ℓ¹ standpoint, with slack factor
  e^{log3-Λ} ≈ 1.88 (model 2). This is the numerical basis of H-127's
  Proposition C — it explains why the Littlewood-Offord/Riesz-product
  technique from the 2011 post doesn't close in the WCC regime, unlike
  the regime where Tao originally uses it (there the
  dimension/log-modulus ratio is unbounded, here it's fixed and small).

## Files

- `experiment_lambda_mc.py` — a single script, two models, exact
  computation of E|Z|² and Monte Carlo estimate of Λ with standard
  error.

## Reproduce

```
python3 experiment_lambda_mc.py
```

Cost: ~1-2s (2×10⁶ samples × 60 terms, vectorized in numpy).
